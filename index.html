Our Story

Sept. 2010

A mutual friend - Rachel Heaton, aka Blade, aka Rayban, aka “bad Rachel” - invites Rachel to go to a book release party. The book is about Ruby, and Rachel thinks, “I love fine minerals!” She wonders why the author, Paul, pays so much attention to her at his party. The next day, they become Facebook friends.

Fall and winter 2010

Paul and Rachel hang out. Rachel drags Paul out to see “Case 39” and to the ice-skating rink; Paul teaches her about NYC karaoke, bodegas and sweet drinking spots. They go bowling and tie two games in a row (scores: 117 and 142). Paul makes a Brazilian feast for Rachel (farofa with bananas, fried yuca with habaneros and white fish with coconut sauce) - the first time he’s ever cooked for lady friend!

March 2011

Paul decides to woo Rachel on a spring break trip. Rachel chooses St. Lucia because it’s a new country and has interesting rock formations. They are both nervous about actually spending more than a few hours together at one time. Things work out: they are upgraded to the honeymoon suite and discover a mutual love of 90s music.

July 2011

Paul introduced Rachel as “his girlfriend” to someone he runs into at a baseball game. How presumptuous! But Rachel does not protest.

July 2011

Rachel visits Denver for the first time, where she meets Paul’s family and friends, sees his old high school and site of his first tech-related job (Computer City!), and samples green chili and bison meat.

September 2011

Paul and Rachel move in together in Williamsburg, Brooklyn. Paul becomes the adoptive father of Rachel’s cat, Kit, aka, the Small Orange Baby. They celebrate with a hipster housewarming party.

May 2012

Rachel takes Paul to Puerto Rico for his birthday. They take a terrifying single-prop, eight-seat flight to Culebra, where they see a sea turtle while snorkeling and meet cool, mangrove-painting locals.

September 2012

They venture to Asia for two weeks. Together, they stalk Komodo dragons and bathe out of a bucket in rural Indonesia. Rachel gets a large scar mountain biking in Hong Kong; Paul gets food poisoning at the airport in Malaysia.

January 2013

Paul drags Rachel across the country for a three-month stint in San Francisco, where he takes part in a startup accelerator called Y Combinator. The Small Orange Baby comes along as Rachel’s psychiatrist-certified “comfort animal,” to get around their new apartment's no-pet regulations.

July 2013

On a two-week trip to Sweden and Norway, they explore a Soviet ghost town in Svalbard, drink under the midnight sun and eat at one of the world’s best restaurants. Scandinavia, they learn, is wildly expensive.

September 2013

Paul takes Rachel to Bermuda “for her birthday.” He gets horribly seasick on a morning scuba diving excursion, and misses the second dive because his ears wouldn’t pop. Undeterred, that evening he proposes to Rachel on their hotel balcony at sunset, with champers! She says yes.

March 2014

They get a civil union so Rachel can piggyback on Paul’s health insurance policy. Romantic!

August 2014

They ride two-humped camels together in the Gobi…

September 2014

They launch this website, and prepare to throw an amazing party for their family and friends!

Bride & Groom

Rachel

Rachel grew up exploring the bayous and beaches of her native Biloxi, Mississippi. Friends often referred to her house as “Noah’s Ark” because of the menagerie of animals found there - chickens, iguanas, hedgehogs, snakes… She and her sister could often be found under a derelict bridge, trapping feral kittens which they tamed and found homes for.

Her love of all thing wild and green led her to study biology at Loyola University New Orleans. After graduating, she headed to Japan for a year to teach English, and then enrolled in an ecology master’s program in Europe. She spent time researching illegal wildlife trade in a peat swamp in Vietnam, where she ate a rat, got attacked by thousands of bees and was thrown off a motorbike. But mid-way through her degree she realized that statistics and data collection weren’t her favorite things, so she applied to NYU’s science journalism master’s program. Writing about science rather than doing science turned out to be a perfect fit - as was New York City. Now, she writes for places such as The New York Times, the BBC and Smithsonian.

When not hunched over her desk rushing to meet a deadline, Rachel loves traveling (she’s been to 40 countries), cooking (she does it to spoil Paul) and taking photos (oftentimes of food, while traveling!).

Amber Williams

Rachel and I met the first day of grad school in 2010, but our friendship really began in a shared cubicle named Biedler Forest, where, as summer interns for Audubon Magazine, we complained about grad school. The struggles—long hours, high costs, rough critiques—are what brought us together. That cubicle is where I first heard about Paul, too, a super smart guy who shared her love of new experiences and, crucially, travel.

Since then, we have danced two New Year’s Eves away, dressed as incarcerated lesbian lovers, and wrapped our arms around R.L. Stine and James Watson - amongst many more times I’ll always remember. And happily, our friendship extends into work. Rachel is an exceptional science journalist and I’m grateful to have her as a writer at Scientific American. Now if only she’d stop trying to set me up with every guy she meets!

Tracey Lee Williams

I met Rachel when she was 18 and I was 33, in a tiny village in France. We did not click right away—that happened months later at a Latin nightclub in Paris. Over the last 10 years, she has become one of my best friends and an amazing travel buddy. We have met up in Mississippi and Manhattan, too many times to count. I visited her in Kiso, Japan, where she taught for the JET Program, and we visited temples in Kyoto. Another year, we had a lovely Thanksgiving dinner of cheese fondue in Poitiers, France, before heading to Paris. And she once came to my rescue in The Middle of Nowhere, Slovenia.

When I first got to know “RachelNuwer,” I told her I wanted to be her when I grew up—she was this incredible 18 year-old! And, now, I feel the same—she’s still incredible, and I still want to be her when I grow up. I love her to pieces!

Best Man

Paul was throwing a party at his place in Denver many years ago, and I met him there through our mutual friend Dave. The only really distinct memory I have from that night was that, after asking what he did for a living, he not only told me what he did but also how good he was at it. Since that wasn't enough, apparently, he brought me to his computer and pulled up an e-mail from a client noting Paul's "god-like abilities." I kept thinking, "Why is this 13-year-old boy so full of himself?" But how wrong I was. He was much older.

Eventually, we wound up working at several jobs together over a few years, including a software startup and at an Air Force base in Colorado Springs. Things got even better when we moved to New York City within a few months of each other. We've had amazing times in New York and in travels to Brazilian beaches, Japanese temples, Mongolian gers and many spots in between.

I suppose there is one thing that is god-like about Paul after all: his friendship.

Brother of the Groom

Paul has always been like a brother to me. It is a relationship that started with mutual dislike but eventually transitioned to mutual respect (at least I think its mutual). The transition from being just an annoying older brother to a person that I respect might have begun on a camping trip near Creede, Colorado with several friends.

It was while I was in college but from my memory of the end of the trip I was definitely of drinking age. We went hiking and backpacking and were going to tackle a random 14er. I had always considered myself the more athletic one of the two of us due to my participation in organized sports and conversely Paul's obsession with the video game Doom II (amongst others). That's why it came as a great surprise to me that day after day he was in the front of the pack during our hikes while I was dragging in the back. At the beginning I found it embarrassing but as the trip continued I felt nothing but pride and respect. And since then the pride and respect has only increased.

Lt. Coulter Reyes

In 1989, I was an impressionable young buck of a mere 10 years. The purpose of the Cherrelyn Elementary School visit to Flood Middle School was simple: match each 5th grader with a wise 6th grader who would guide their young protégé in the erudite path of 6th graderness. That is… unless you had the misfortune of being paired with Mr. Paul Dix. As my mentor, Paul ridiculed me for my selection of “pizza” as my favorite food and “red” as my favorite color. But no insult would sting as painfully as my ill-fated attempt at humor: Trying to demonstrate my savoir faire as a 6th grade candidate, I jested that my favorite band was “NKOTB.” There was a moment of startling silence, and then a twisted grimace slowly spread over Paul’s pre-pubescent maw. Chortling, he bellowed for all to hear: “THIS GUY LIKES NKOTB!!!” Needless to say, the resulting cacophony of adolescent laughter scarred me for life. To this day, I dare not utter a jest to try “to be cool.”

THANK YOU PAUL FOR RUINING MY CHILDHOOD! All jokes aside, you’ve been my best friend for decades and I owe it all to your disdain for the NKOTB (as I too loathed the NKOTB-laden airways of 1989). I HEART PAULIE!

Robert Reyes

I met Paul back in the good old early nineties, around 7th grade. My brother and I were living in my Grandpa’s house over on S. Pearl and Yale in Denver. We started hanging out with a neighborhood boy, Geoff, who lived down the block. Well, as I’m sure you all know, Geoff’s big bro is the one and only Pauly! Skip forward a few years to high school, and guess who was in the high school band? None other than P himself!

Thus began a friendship that ended up being lifelong. Our summers were filled with volleyball matches, long days on our skates and far more soda than any human alive should ever consume. After high school, we roommated together for years. This was an era fueled by Baz Luhrman, luaus at our house and (family, please ignore this last blurb), enough beer bottles in our recycling bin to rebuild the Taj Mahal. When I recollect on my youth, my fondest memories are, by far, those endless summers that we spent skating, roving the grounds of Elitch's amusement park and pulling all-nighters playing video games. Congratulations Paul! You deserve every ounce of happiness coming your way!

Dave Lawrence
